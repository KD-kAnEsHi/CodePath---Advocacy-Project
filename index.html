The PolTech Manifesto

To address the low voter amongs teenagers between the age of 18 to 29 years old and the low Income individuals in local, state, and federal elections. Young Americans, have consistently shown lower voter turnout compared to older age groups, especailly when it comes to local elections despite the fact that local election arguiably impacts us and our society more than the federal elections. This sharp gape is due to the fact that young voters often face obstacles such as complex voter registration rules, lack of information on where and how to vote, and challenges in navigating the voting process. This can lead to a lack of follow-through despite their interest and intentions to participat in the elections. This is where PolTech comes in. We are a group of young voters who are dedicate to inspire young Americans and low-income individuals to actively participate in shaping their future through the democratic process. Together, we aim to break down the barriers that hinder voter engagement, promoting a more inclusive and representative democracy for all. Join us in making a difference, one vote at a time.

Our Mission is Simple and Impactfull

Our mission is to providing an easily accessible and unbiased platform where individuals can inform themselves about local candidates, issues, and the importance of various positions. This website aims to empower voters with the knowledge needed to make informed decisions, ultimately fostering increased civic engagement and representation.
